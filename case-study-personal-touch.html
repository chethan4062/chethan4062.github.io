01 — The Brief

What I was handed, and what I was asked to build.

Personal Touch Skincare came to Insane Labs with a clear goal: scale D2C sales profitably. Here's the account I inherited and the challenge I was set.

Brand
Personal Touch Skincare (PTS) — skincarepersonaltouch.com
Founders
Ashish Jawa (12+ yrs pharma expertise) & Aditi Jawa (luxury branding, ex-Salvatore Ferragamo)
Positioning
'Every skin needs a personal touch' — science-backed, globally-patented ingredients formulated for Indian skin. Patented actives like SabiWhite and Clair Blanche II, Korean-inspired snail mucin range, dermatologically tested, cruelty-free, vegan, sulfate-free, paraben-free.
Scale at Handover
3.5 Lakh+ customers. Shopify D2C site + Nykaa, Amazon, Flipkart, Swiggy Instamart. Price range ₹249–₹1,399.

Key Commercial Levers Available
Aggressive combo pricing: Buy Any 3 at ₹899 · Buy Any 5 at ₹2,499 · 7-day money-back guarantee · Concern-based collections (acne, pigmentation, anti-ageing, eczema, rosacea, open pores) · GoKwik fast checkout · COD available
The competitive context I was working in: Personal Touch was competing directly against Minimalist, Mamaearth, Dot & Key, and Plum — all with larger brand recognition and bigger marketing budgets. The edge I had to find wasn't budget; it was precision — better creative, smarter segmentation, and a funnel architecture that made the math work at scale.

What I was tasked with

  • Scale D2C sales through Meta and Google — from ₹30L to ₹60L/month in managed ad spend over 3 months
  • Build a full-funnel acquisition strategy from cold prospecting to retargeting to post-purchase retention — not just run ads
  • Drive profitable ROAS — not just volume. The target was 4x blended ROAS at scale
  • Reduce CAC through continuous creative testing and audience refinement — CAC was a key reporting metric to the client
  • Increase AOV — single-product purchases at ₹349 don't make the unit economics work at ₹50L/month spend. Combo offer adoption was a KPI I owned
  • Improve prepaid order ratio — COD orders with 25–30% RTO rates were bleeding true ROAS. Shifting the ratio was part of my brief
  • Compete effectively against established competitors with stronger brand recognition and larger overall budgets
02 — My Strategy

How I approached it before spending a rupee.

The first thing I did wasn't launch ads. It was understand exactly where the account was wasting money and build the infrastructure to spend smarter.

🔍
Account Audit & Infrastructure Setup
  • Meta Pixel audit: identified missing and misfiring events — ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, and Purchase weren't all firing correctly with value parameters. Fixed this before scaling spend, as inaccurate data was poisoning the algorithm's optimization.
  • Campaign consolidation: inherited 12+ fragmented campaigns, multiple overlapping ad sets, and duplicated audiences — a classic sign of an account that was built reactively, not strategically. I consolidated to 3 core campaigns so Meta's algorithm could learn from pooled data rather than competing against itself.
  • Google conversion tracking: implemented enhanced conversions for web on the Shopify store. Set up Google Merchant Center and optimized the product feed with keyword-rich titles, accurate pricing, and high-quality images.
  • Audience architecture: built a complete audience stack before touching creative — website visitors (7/14/30/60/90 day windows), cart abandoners, past purchasers segmented by recency and product, email list custom audiences, and lookalikes at 1%, 2%, 5% seeded from purchase events.
  • Exclusion mapping: set up proper exclusion logic — past purchasers excluded from cold prospecting, recent buyers excluded from retargeting. Obvious in theory, rarely done correctly in practice.

Meta Campaigns I Built
  • Advantage+ Shopping Campaign (ASC) as the scaling engine: consolidated the majority of prospecting budget into 2 ASC campaigns — one for hero products (Facebath, Sunstalker, Youthburst), one for the full catalogue. Fed diverse creative and let Meta's algorithm identify buyers at scale. ASC's broad reach paired with strong creative outperformed manual interest stacking at high spend levels.
  • Creative testing campaign (10–15% of budget): dedicated budget to testing 8–10 new creatives per week. Winners identified in 3–5 days by CTR + cost-per-ATC threshold before graduating to the scaling campaign. This kept the scaling campaign fed with proven creative and maintained CPM efficiency.
  • Retargeting campaign — 3 segmented ad sets: (1) cart abandoners 0–7 days with urgency messaging and 7-day money-back guarantee, (2) product page viewers 7–14 days with social proof and review highlights, (3) Instagram/Facebook engagers 14–30 days with combo offer creative. Each segment got distinct creative and messaging — not the same ad to everyone.
  • Dynamic Product Ads (DPA): showing the exact product someone browsed with real pricing, star ratings, and review counts pulled from the product catalogue. High-relevance retargeting that consistently delivered 8–12x ROAS in this segment.
  • Retention campaign: past purchaser audiences segmented by last purchase date and product category. New launch ads, cross-sell bundles ('bought sunscreen → show moisturizer'), and replenishment reminders at 30–45 day intervals for consumable products.
🔍
Google Ads — Capturing Demand Meta Created
  • Brand search protection: competitors were actively bidding on 'personal touch skincare', 'sunstalker sunscreen', 'facebath facewash'. I locked these down and delivered 10x+ ROAS — these campaigns were the highest-efficiency spend on the account and non-negotiable at any budget level.
  • Google Shopping: product feed optimized with keyword-rich titles (e.g., 'Facebath Gentle Facewash for Oily Skin — Niacinamide, SLS-free, 100ml'), competitive pricing, and review count visible in listing. Shopping campaigns captured high-intent product-specific searches and delivered 4–5x ROAS.
  • Non-brand search: bidding on 'best sunscreen for oily skin India', 'niacinamide face wash', 'snail mucin serum India', 'dark spot corrector for face'. These drove new customer acquisition at 2–3x ROAS — lower than brand search but strategically necessary to expand reach beyond the existing audience pool.
  • Performance Max: fed with Meta-proven creative assets and purchase-event audience signals. Treated as a supplementary reach campaign — useful for filling gaps in Google's reach at modest budget allocation.
03 — Campaign Execution

What I actually did, week after week.

Strategy is easy. Execution at ₹60L/month — where every wasted rupee costs real money — is where the work actually happens.

Creative Framework I Built

I developed a systematic testing framework: 8–10 new creatives per week, identified winners in 3–5 days based on CTR and cost-per-ATC, graduated winners to the scaling campaign, and rotated 60–70% of the creative library every 2–3 weeks to combat frequency-driven fatigue.

Funnel & Conversion Optimizations I Owned
  • Landing page strategy: cold traffic went to the bestsellers collection page, retargeting traffic went to the exact product page they viewed, offer campaigns went to the combo deal landing page. Matching landing page to ad intent reduced bounce rate and improved purchase conversion rate.
  • AOV push with combo offers: 'Buy Any 3 at ₹899' was the single most impactful lever I pulled. It increased AOV from ~₹399 to ₹649+ and made the unit economics of ₹50L/month Meta spend viable. I structured ad creative and landing pages to make combos the default CTA, not an afterthought.
  • GoKwik checkout integration: worked with the brand to enable GoKwik for faster, trust-verified checkout. Reducing checkout friction at ₹60L/month spend translated to meaningful revenue recovery.
  • Prepaid incentivization: implemented 'FREE wipes on all prepaid orders above ₹750' — shifted prepaid ratio from 45% to 68%. With 25–30% COD RTO eating into true ROAS, this single initiative improved effective ROAS by an estimated 12–15%.
  • Scaling approach: used horizontal scaling — duplicating winning ad sets with slight creative variations rather than increasing budgets (which triggers algorithm re-learning). Daily CPA monitoring at ad set level with a 48-hour pause rule for anything exceeding 1.5x target CPA.
04 — Funnel & Creative Architecture

The full structure I built and managed.

Every rupee had a job. Here's the exact funnel architecture I designed — how budget was allocated, what creative ran where, and what each stage was optimized for.

Cold Prospecting · Meta
Awareness & New Customer Acquisition
55–60% of Meta budget · ~₹28–30L/mo
+
  • Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC): primary scaling engine. Broad targeting (India, 18–45, female) — fed with diverse creative library and let Meta's AI find buyers. Consolidated from 12+ campaigns into 2 ASC campaigns: one for hero products, one for full catalogue.
  • Interest-based ad sets: tested stacks including skincare enthusiasts, K-beauty fans, dermatology followers, competitor brand interests (Minimalist, The Ordinary, Dot & Key). Winning interest combinations moved into the ASC audience signal.
  • Lookalike audiences: 1% purchase LAL (highest quality), 2% ATC LAL, 5% website visitor LAL. Built from accurate pixel data post-audit. 1% purchase LAL was the highest-converting prospecting audience at scale.
  • Creative for this stage: educational Reels (ingredient science, founder story), product-hero statics with ingredient callouts, concern-based ad sets (acne/pigmentation/anti-ageing separately). Goal was relevance, not just reach.
  • Goal: drive website traffic and add-to-carts at target CPM. ROAS expectation at this stage: 2.5–3x — knowingly below overall target, as this stage fills the retargeting pool that profits the whole funnel.
Retargeting · Meta
Warm Audience Conversion — The Profit Centre
25–30% of Meta budget · ~₹12–15L/mo
+
  • Cart abandoners (0–7 days): urgency-driven creative — 'Still thinking? Your cart is waiting' + '7-day money-back guarantee' + limited stock messaging. Highest priority segment. Creative featured exact product left in cart alongside social proof.
  • Product page viewers (7–14 days): social proof-driven — review compilations, '3.5L+ customers trust us', before-after UGC for specific products. Star ratings and review counts front and centre. Also ran DPA (Dynamic Product Ads) showing exact products browsed with real pricing.
  • Instagram/Facebook engagers (14–30 days): offer-driven — 'Buy Any 3 at ₹899' combo deals, first-purchase incentives, collection page ads. These were warm but not product-specific, so offer creative with broad appeal worked best.
  • Dynamic Product Ads (DPA): running continuously across all retargeting segments — product image, price, star rating, review count pulled dynamically from catalogue. Consistently delivered 8–12x ROAS. Best-performing segment on the entire account.
  • Goal: convert warm audiences into paying customers at 8–12x ROAS. This segment subsidized prospecting and is where the account's profitability lived.
Google Ads
High-Intent Demand Capture — ₹10L/mo
₹10L/mo — 100% Google
+
  • Brand Search (35% of Google budget): protected 'personal touch skincare', 'PTS skincare', 'sunstalker sunscreen', 'facebath facewash' — competitors were bidding on these terms. Brand search delivered 10x+ ROAS and was non-negotiable at any budget level. Every Meta impression that drives brand recognition needs a Google brand ad to capture the resulting search.
  • Google Shopping (30%): product feed with keyword-rich titles, competitive pricing, and review ratings visible. Captured high-intent product searches — 'SPF 50 sunscreen oily skin', 'niacinamide facewash India'. Delivered 4–5x ROAS with high purchase intent conversion.
  • Non-Brand Search (20%): generic high-intent terms: 'best sunscreen for oily skin India', 'snail mucin serum India', 'dark spot corrector for face'. Lower ROAS (2–3x) but strategically important for acquiring customers who haven't yet been exposed to Meta ads.
  • Performance Max (15%): fed with proven creative from Meta, audience signals from purchase event data. Supplementary reach — improving efficiency over time as audience signals accumulated. Treated as the Google equivalent of Meta's Advantage+ at small scale.
  • The Meta-Google relationship: Meta created demand; Google captured it. The 83/17 spend split (₹50L Meta, ₹10L Google) was deliberate — every Meta impression that reaches someone who then Googles the brand converts via brand search at near-zero CPC. The two channels have to be planned together.
Retention & Cross-sell · Meta
Past Purchaser LTV Maximisation
10–15% of Meta budget · ~₹5–7L/mo
+
  • Cross-sell sequences: bought sunscreen → show moisturizer at a combo price. Bought facewash → show serum. Bought Facebath → show the 'complete routine' bundle. Matched based on product category purchased and time since last order.
  • Replenishment reminders: automated at 30–45 days post-purchase for consumable products. 'Running low on Facebath? Reorder and save 15%' — timed to the product's likely usage cycle. Delivered 5–6x ROAS at minimal incremental spend.
  • New product launch ads: past purchasers were the first audience for every new SKU launch. Existing customers convert on new products at dramatically higher rates than cold audiences — and trust the brand enough to try something unfamiliar.
  • Bundle/routine upsell: 'Complete your skincare routine' — facewash + sunscreen + moisturizer combo at ₹1,299. Higher AOV and higher LTV per customer. Positioned as a routine recommendation, not a hard upsell.
  • Goal: increase repeat purchase rate and LTV. 5–6x ROAS with minimal incremental spend — this was the highest-efficiency segment on the account after DPA retargeting.

Key Optimizations That Moved the Needle

  • 01
    Advantage+ Campaign Consolidation
    Consolidating from 12+ fragmented campaigns to 2–3 ASC campaigns improved Meta's algorithm efficiency by giving it more data per campaign to optimize against. The algorithm learns faster with pooled budget — CPM dropped 18% within 3 weeks of consolidation.
    ↓ 18% CPM reduction
  • 02
    Creative Diversity Protocol
    Ensuring no two ads in the same ad set looked visually alike — mixing statics, videos, carousels, and UGC within each campaign. Creative diversity is the primary lever against CPM inflation from frequency fatigue at high spend levels. Combined with a 2–3 week rotation cycle, this kept CPMs stable as spend scaled from ₹30L to ₹60L/month.
    CPM stability maintained through scale-up
  • 03
    Combo Offer AOV Strategy
    The 'Buy Any 3 at ₹899' offer restructured the entire unit economics of the account. At ₹399 AOV, a 4x ROAS target requires a ₹100 CPA — extremely tight at scale. At ₹649 AOV, the same ROAS target allows ₹162 CPA — far more achievable. This wasn't just a promotions decision; it was a fundamental change to what performance marketing could achieve profitably.
    ↑ 63% AOV · ₹399 → ₹649
  • 04
    Prepaid Incentivization
    COD orders in Indian D2C carry 25–30% RTO rates. An order that returns has negative ROAS — you've paid for the customer acquisition AND the return logistics. Shifting from 45% to 68% prepaid ratio through the 'free wipes on prepaid' offer improved true ROAS (accounting for returns) by an estimated 12–15%, without changing a single ad or targeting parameter.
    ↑ 23pp prepaid ratio · ~12–15% true ROAS improvement
  • 05
    Concern-Based Creative Segmentation
    Running separate creative for acne-prone, pigmentation-concerned, and dry skin audiences — each matched to the relevant concern-based product collection page on the Shopify site. Ad relevance score improvement translated directly to lower CPCs and higher CTR. A generic skincare ad talks to everyone; a pigmentation-specific ad with Melakey before-after talks to exactly the person who needs it.
    ↑ 40% CTR improvement vs generic ads
06 — What I Learned

What managing ₹60L/month taught me.

Five things I know now that I wish I'd known at the start — and that I carry into every account I manage.

  • 01
    At scale, creative is the media buy
    At ₹60L/month, I spent about 80% of my strategic thinking on creative and 20% on media buying. Meta's algorithm at this spend level is smart enough to find buyers if you feed it good creative. The constraint is almost never targeting or bidding — it's creative quality, diversity, and refresh rate. I became a creative strategist who also happened to manage a media budget.
  • 02
    AOV is the silent ROAS killer — and fixer
    In a category where products cost ₹299–₹599, you cannot profitably acquire customers on single-product purchases at scale. The math simply doesn't work. Combo offers weren't a sales gimmick — they were a unit economics necessity. I now evaluate every new D2C account's AOV potential before I look at any targeting or creative, because nothing else matters as much to whether profitable scale is achievable.
  • 03
    Google is the net, Meta is the fishing rod
    Meta creates demand; Google captures it. The 83/17 spend split (₹50L Meta, ₹10L Google) wasn't arbitrary — it reflected where each channel delivered value in the funnel. Every Meta impression that builds brand awareness generates branded search queries that convert at 10x+ ROAS on Google brand campaigns. Planning both channels together as a system is fundamentally different from managing them separately.
  • 04
    Retargeting is the profit centre; prospecting is the investment
    Prospecting at scale will almost always deliver thin or below-target ROAS. That's not a problem — it's the design. Prospecting's job is to fill the retargeting pool with warm audiences who will then convert at 8–12x ROAS. I structured the entire budget and reporting framework around this reality. When leadership sees prospecting ROAS, I show them retargeting ROAS alongside it. The whole system has to be evaluated together.
  • 05
    Prepaid vs COD is a performance marketing problem, not just ops
    In Indian D2C, a COD order with 25–30% RTO effectively cuts your ROAS in half on that segment. The prepaid incentivization strategy I implemented was as impactful to true ROAS as any ad optimization I ran. Performance marketers who ignore the post-click, post-purchase journey are optimizing an incomplete system. The ad's job isn't done at the purchase — it's done when the product is delivered and kept.
